movement, not empire

We are not the first to care. Good.

The data sovereignty movement exists, and it is bigger than any one project. Our stance: join it, don't dominate it. Adopt existing standards where they fit. Contribute where they fall short. The ego move is to build everything from scratch and claim the credit. The strategic move is to strengthen the ecosystem that strengthens us.

An honest note before the list: none of these people or projects endorse dreamtree, and most have never heard of us. We name them because we build on their work, cite their evidence, and share their fight — and because the mission is bigger than any organization, including this one.

The standards we build on

W3C Verifiable Credentials & DIDs

standard · implemented in roots

The credential and identifier standards our wallet speaks natively: VC 2.0 with data-integrity proofs, DID resolution anyone can perform. We did not invent the trust format. We implemented it faithfully.

1EdTech Open Badges

standard · implemented

The education world's credential standard. Certificates issued in our ecosystem follow Open Badges 3.0, so they verify in anyone's tooling, not just ours.

C2PA

standard · content provenance

The Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity embeds origin and edit history into media — now shipping in Leica and Nikon cameras, Adobe and OpenAI services, BBC images. The same fight as ours, fought at the level of pixels.

Decentralized Identity Foundation

community

The engineering commons where portable identity is being worked out in public. Where our needs outgrow current specs, this is where we take them.

Fellow travelers

The Solid Project

project · tim berners-lee

Personal data stores from the inventor of the web: your data in pods you control, applications that come to the data instead of hoarding it. A different architecture aimed at the same sovereignty.

AT Protocol / Bluesky

project · portable identity at scale

Proof that account portability works for tens of millions of people: your identity and social graph travel with you. What they did for the social graph, we are doing for the record of accomplishment.

MyData Global

movement

A declaration of human-centric data rights translated into 13 languages, with fingerprints on the Finnish Government Programme and the European Data Strategy. The policy arm of the same idea.

RadicalxChange

movement · data as labor

The community formalizing what fair compensation for data could actually look like — the economics we deliberately refuse to dictate alone.

The thinkers whose work we stand on

Shoshana Zuboff

diagnosis

Named surveillance capitalism: human experience claimed unilaterally as free raw material for behavioral prediction. Part I of our case rests on her shoulders.

Jaron Lanier

principle

Data dignity: what you create belongs to you, and the exchange between people and platforms should be transparent and consensual. Our wallet is that principle, shipped.

Mariana Mazzucato

economics

Taught the difference between creating value and extracting it — and how thoroughly modern platforms confused the two.

Elinor Ostrom

governance

Proved that commons can be governed — Nobel-winning fieldwork from fisheries to a Swiss Alpine commons run by farmers since 1517. Her principles shape how the library will be governed.

Proof that generosity scales

Wikipedia & the free software movement

existence proof

The largest reference work in human history, free. An operating system built by volunteers running 96% of the top million web servers. Contribution compounds.

Signal & Mozilla

existence proof

Mission-governed technology serving hundreds of millions without harvesting them. You do not have to choose between reach and principles.

Purpose Foundation & steward-ownership

structure · ours too

The legal architecture we are adopting: profits stay in the mission, no sale to extractive buyers, control stays with stewards. Ecosia, Patagonia, and most of the Copenhagen stock market's value already run on it. Danish steward-owned firms show six times higher survival odds at 40 years.

ASCAP & BMI

existence proof · a century old

Over $1 billion a year distributed to songwriters from verified play data. Provenance-to-compensation is not a hypothesis. Music has run it for a hundred years.

Build with us. Build alongside us. Build something better and prove us wrong. The goal is not that dreamtree wins. The goal is that the contribution economy exists.
